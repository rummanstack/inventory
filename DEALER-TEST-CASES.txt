STOCKLEDGER DEALER FEATURE TEST CASES
Date: 2026-07-13

Automated command:
npm run test:dealer-check

Build command:
npm --prefix frontend run build

Database requirement:
Use a disposable local or dedicated PostgreSQL test database in backend/.env as DEV_DATABASE_URL.
Do not run dealer automation against a production/shared Supabase pooler database.

============================================================
1. INVENTORY CORE
============================================================

TC-INV-001 Products - Create Product
Steps:
1. Create a product with category, pieces per case, purchase price, wholesale price, retail price, and reorder level.
2. Fetch product list.
Expected:
Product is created, appears in list, and calculated/display fields are correct.

TC-INV-002 Products - Edit Product Without Direct Stock Mutation
Steps:
1. Create product.
2. Try updating normal fields.
3. Try sending stockPieces directly in update payload.
Expected:
Normal fields update. Direct stockPieces mutation is rejected.

TC-INV-003 Stock Movement - Add Stock
Steps:
1. Create product.
2. Add stock with reason.
3. Open stock movement for product.
Expected:
Product stock increases. Stock movement has MANUAL_ADJUSTMENT, quantity in, reference/note, and correct balance after.

TC-INV-004 Low Stock Alerts
Steps:
1. Create one product below reorder level.
2. Create one product above reorder level.
3. Open low stock alerts.
Expected:
Only below-threshold product appears.

TC-INV-005 Product Serials
Steps:
1. Create serial-required product.
2. Add serial/IMEI.
3. Try duplicate serial/IMEI.
4. Try adding serial to non-serial product.
Expected:
Valid serial is accepted. Duplicate and non-serial-product serial creation are rejected.

TC-INV-006 Damaged Stock
Steps:
1. Create product with stock.
2. Record damaged quantity through settlement or damaged stock flow.
3. Clear part/all damaged stock.
Expected:
Sellable/damaged stock counts are correct and stock movement/history remains traceable.

============================================================
2. DSR AND DEALER FIELD SALES
============================================================

TC-DSR-001 DSR CRUD
Steps:
1. Create DSR with name, phone, area, and opening due.
2. Edit DSR.
3. List/search DSR.
Expected:
DSR is created, updated, searchable, and opening due creates ledger entry.

TC-DSR-002 DSR Opening Due Change Requires Reason
Steps:
1. Create DSR with opening due.
2. Change opening due without reason.
3. Change opening due with reason.
Expected:
Change without reason is rejected. Change with reason updates due ledger correctly.

TC-DSR-003 Morning Issue
Steps:
1. Create product with stock.
2. Create DSR.
3. Create morning issue for product quantity.
Expected:
Product stock decreases. MORNING_ISSUE stock movement is created. Over-issue is rejected.

TC-DSR-004 Settlement
Steps:
1. Create morning issue.
2. Settle with sold quantity, returned quantity, damaged quantity, cash collection, discount, and due.
Expected:
Returned stock goes back to stock. Damaged stock updates. Cash/finance balance updates. DSR due ledger is correct.

4. SUPPLIERS AND PURCHASES
============================================================

TC-SUP-001 Supplier CRUD
Steps:
1. Create supplier with name, phone, address, opening due.
2. Edit supplier.
3. Search/list.
Expected:
Supplier is created, updated, searchable, and opening due becomes current due.

TC-PUR-001 Purchase Receive Fully Paid
Steps:
1. Fund cash account.
2. Create product and supplier.
3. Receive purchase as fully paid.
Expected:
Stock increases. PURCHASE_RECEIVE movement is created. Supplier due is cleared. Cash decreases.

TC-PUR-002 Purchase Receive Partial Payment
Steps:
1. Create purchase with partial payment.
2. Check supplier due ledger.
Expected:
Remaining payable appears as supplier due.

TC-PUR-003 Purchase Return
Steps:
1. Receive purchase.
2. Return part of purchase.
3. Check stock movement and supplier ledger.
Expected:
Stock decreases. PURCHASE_RETURN movement is created. Supplier due/advance is adjusted correctly.

TC-SUPPAY-001 Supplier Payment
Steps:
1. Create supplier due.
2. Fund cash account.
3. Record supplier payment.
Expected:
Cash decreases. Supplier due decreases. Overpayment becomes supplier advance if supported.

TC-SUPDISC-001 Supplier Discount
Steps:
1. Create supplier due.
2. Record supplier discount.
Expected:
Supplier due decreases without stock movement.

TC-SUPSTAT-001 Supplier Statement
Steps:
1. Create purchase, return, payment, and discount for supplier.
2. Open supplier statement for date range.
Expected:
Opening balance, movements, totals, and closing balance match source records.

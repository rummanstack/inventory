Recommended navigation:

---

# 1. Purpose

This module is for retailer-managed monthly installment sales.

It is like EMI, but not bank EMI. The shop sells products to a registered customer, collects an optional down payment, adds optional markup or discount, then creates a monthly payment schedule.

Example:

Product total: 50,000
Down payment: 10,000
Remaining amount: 40,000
Markup: 10%
Markup amount: 4,000
Installment payable: 44,000
Duration: 8 months
Monthly installment: 5,500
First payment date: 2026-08-10
Payment day: 10th of every month

---

# 2. Core Flow

1. Retailer creates a new installment sale.
2. Retailer selects a registered retail customer.
3. Retailer adds products.
4. System calculates product subtotal, discount, tax if any, and sale total.
5. Retailer enters down payment.
6. Retailer enters markup percentage or fixed markup amount.
7. Retailer may enter discount.
8. Retailer selects number of months.
9. Retailer selects first payment date.
10. System previews monthly schedule.
11. Retailer confirms.
12. System creates sale invoice, stock movement, installment plan, and installment schedule.
13. Retailer collects installments monthly.

# 5. Accounting / Ledger Behavior

## 5.1 On Installment Sale Creation

Effects:
- Stock decreases immediately.
- Sale invoice is created.
- Down payment increases cash/bank.
- Installment receivable is created for remaining payable.
- Customer installment outstanding increases.

Important decision:
Markup income can be recognized in two ways.

Option A: Recognize full markup at sale creation.
- Easier.
- But profit may look high before money is collected.

Option B: Recognize markup gradually as installments are collected.
- More accurate and safer.
- Recommended.

Recommendation:
Use Option B. Recognize markup income proportionally with each installment payment.

## 5.2 On Installment Payment

Effects:
- Cash/bank increases.
- Installment receivable decreases.
- Markup income is recognized proportionally if using gradual recognition.
- Schedule rows update to PAID/PARTIAL.

## 5.3 On Early Full Settlement

Effects:
- Customer pays all remaining amount.
- All unpaid schedules become PAID.
- Plan becomes COMPLETED.
- Optional settlement discount may be allowed.

## 5.4 On Reschedule

Rules:
- Require reason.
- Keep old schedule history.
- Create adjustment/reschedule audit entry.
- Do not silently overwrite paid schedule rows.

## 5.5 On Cancel Plan

Rules:
- Require reason.
- Only allow cancellation if business rules are safe.
- If product already returned, reverse stock/sale carefully.
- If payments already collected, define refund or adjustment behavior.

Recommendation:
For first version, allow cancellation only when no installment payment has been collected except down payment, or require manual admin handling.

# 6. Customer Impact

Retail customer profile should show:
- Active installment count
- Total installment outstanding
- Overdue installment amount
- Next payment date
- Installment payment history
- Products bought through installment

Customer due ledger should remain separate from installment ledger unless intentionally merged.

Recommendation:
Keep normal customer due and installment due separate, but show both in customer summary.

Example customer summary:
- Normal Current Due
- Installment Outstanding
- Total Receivable

# 8. Business Rules

Recommended rules:
- Installment sale must use registered retail customer.
- Walk-in customer cannot use installment.
- Product stock is reduced immediately after installment sale confirmation.
- Down payment is optional or required based on shop policy.
- Markup can be percent or fixed amount.
- Discount can be applied before markup.
- Payment schedule is auto-generated.
- Payment applies to oldest due installment first.
- Partial payment is allowed.
- Extra payment rolls forward to next installment.
- Overdue status is calculated from due date and remaining amount.
- Reschedule requires reason.
- Cancel requires reason.
- Delete should be restricted; prefer cancel/void with audit history.

# 10. Suggested Test Cases

## TC-INST-001 - Create Installment Sale

Steps:

2. Create product with stock.
3. Create installment sale with down payment, markup, months, and first due date.

Expected:
- Invoice is created.
- Stock decreases.
- Down payment enters cash/bank.

Steps:

- Due dates are monthly.

Steps:
1. Create installment plan.
2. Pay less than first installment.

Expected:
- First schedule row becomes PARTIAL.
- Paid amount updates.
- Remaining amount updates.
- Plan outstanding decreases.

## TC-INST-004 - Full Installment Payment

Steps:
1. Pay exact first installment amount.

Expected:
- First schedule row becomes PAID.
- Plan total paid increases.
- Outstanding decreases.

## TC-INST-005 - Extra Payment Allocation

Steps:
1. Pay more than one installment amount.

Expected:
- Oldest unpaid installment is paid first.
- Extra amount applies to next installment.

## TC-INST-006 - Overdue Report

Steps:
1. Create plan with past due date.
2. Do not pay.
3. Open overdue report.

Expected:
- Installment appears as overdue.
- Days overdue and overdue amount are correct.

## TC-INST-007 - Early Full Settlement

Steps:
1. Create active plan.
2. Pay all remaining amount.

Expected:
- All schedules become PAID.
- Plan becomes COMPLETED.

## TC-INST-008 - Customer Installment Statement

Steps:
1. Create multiple installment plans for one customer.

3. Open customer statement.

Expected:
- All plans appear.

Steps:
1. Try to reschedule without reason.

Expected:

# 11. Recommendation

This is a high-value feature for phone, electronics, appliance, and retail shops where customers buy on monthly payments.

However, it touches many critical systems:
- Sales invoice
- Stock
- Customer due
- Finance accounts
- Profit
- Reports
- Customer retention
- Audit history

Recommendation:
Build it as a separate Retail Installments module, not as a small field inside normal sales invoice.

Keep normal customer due and installment due separate internally, then show both in customer summary.

Dealer/DSR Business Management System Gap Analysis
Date: 2026-06-11
Project: Arinda Inventory System

Current Scope
-------------
The project is already a solid Dealer/DSR inventory and settlement MVP.

Implemented areas include:
- Product stock management
- DSR master management
- Morning issue flow
- Evening settlement flow
- Product return and damaged stock tracking
- Expense management
- DSR night cash receipt tracking
- DSR advance tracking
- Month-end DSR summary
- Profit and loss report
- Dashboard and daily reports
- History pages for issue and settlement records
- Activity logs
- User login
- Role and permission control
- Super admin, platform admin, and system developer roles
- Multi-tenant organization support
- Tenant feature toggles
- Organization settings
- Database backup
- System health and error logs
- Print/PDF support
- English and Bangla language support

Build Status
------------
The frontend production build passes.

Note:
There are no automated test scripts in the backend or frontend package files, so a full business-rule regression test suite could not be run.


Main Missing Business Modules
-----------------------------

1. Customer / Outlet Management

The system does not yet have customer, shop, or outlet management.

Missing:
- Customer/outlet master list
- Customer phone, address, market, and route assignment
- Customer credit limit
- Customer opening balance
- Customer due balance
- Customer-wise sales history
- Customer visit frequency
- Customer status
- Location or GPS tagging

Why it matters:
A real dealer business usually tracks sales and dues not only by DSR, but also by shop/customer.


2. Supplier and Purchase Management

Stock is currently added directly, but there is no purchase workflow.

Missing:
- Supplier master
- Purchase order
- Purchase invoice
- Goods receive note
- Purchase return
- Supplier payment
- Supplier due
- Supplier-wise purchase history

Why it matters:
Without purchase records, inventory increases are not connected to real supplier bills or payable tracking.


3. Stock Movement Ledger

The system updates product stock correctly in daily flows, but there is no complete stock ledger.

Missing:
- Stock movement table
- Opening stock entries
- Purchase receive entries
- Manual adjustment entries
- Morning issue entries
- Settlement return entries
- Damaged stock entries
- Extra return entries
- Balance-after-movement tracking
- User and timestamp for every movement

Why it matters:
A real inventory system must answer: why did stock change, who changed it, and what was the balance after the transaction?


4. Automatic Due Ledger

The settlement flow supports previous due, paid amount, and due amount, but previous due is manually entered.

Missing:
- DSR/customer receivable ledger
- Automatic carry-forward of previous due
- Opening balance
- Due collection entries
- Due adjustment entries
- Aging report
- Statement report
- DSR-wise and customer-wise balance

Why it matters:
Manual previous due creates risk of wrong balances and weak month-end control.


5. Cash and Bank Book

The system tracks settlement paid amounts, cash receipts, advances, and expenses, but not a full cash/bank book.

Missing:
- Cash drawer
- Opening cash
- Cash in
- Cash out
- Bank deposit
- Bank withdrawal
- Payment method
- Cheque tracking
- bKash/Nagad/mobile banking tracking
- Daily cash closing
- Cash reconciliation

Why it matters:
Dealer businesses need to match physical cash, bank deposits, DSR collection, expenses, and owner withdrawals.


6. Invoice, Challan, and Receipt Numbering

The system has printable sheets, but no formal numbering system.

Missing:
- Delivery challan number
- Settlement/invoice number
- Money receipt number
- Purchase invoice number
- Sequential document numbering
- Prefix by organization or year
- Reprint tracking

Why it matters:
Real business documents need audit-safe numbers for accounting, disputes, and compliance.


7. Approval, Locking, and Closing Controls

Users can create and update operational records, but there is no strong closing workflow.

Missing:
- Day close
- Month close
- Settlement lock after approval
- Manager approval
- Reopen approval
- Edit reason
- Locked period protection
- Approval logs

Why it matters:
After accounts are checked, old data should not be freely editable without approval.


8. Soft Delete and Trash

Important records are currently hard deleted in repository code.

Missing:
- Soft delete flag
- Deleted by
- Deleted at
- Delete reason
- Trash view
- Restore option
- Permanent delete only for highest admin role

Why it matters:
Hard delete is risky for business records because audit history and recovery become weak.


9. Historical Profit Accuracy

Profit is calculated using the current product purchase price.

Missing:
- Cost captured at sale/settlement time
- Purchase batch costing
- Weighted average cost or FIFO costing
- Historical cost preservation

Why it matters:
If purchase price changes later, old profit reports can become inaccurate.


10. DSR Performance, Salary, and Commission

The system tracks DSR sales and finance basics, but not performance management.

Missing:
- DSR sales target
- Route target
- Commission rules
- Incentive calculation
- Salary ledger
- Attendance
- Leave
- Route productivity
- DSR-wise collection efficiency

Why it matters:
Dealer owners often need to evaluate DSR performance and pay salary/commission correctly.


11. Product Master Depth

The product model is simple and works for current inventory needs, but real dealer operations may need more product details.

Missing:
- SKU code
- Barcode
- Brand
- Pack size
- Unit type
- MRP
- Trade price
- Tax/VAT
- Reorder level
- Batch number
- Expiry date
- Scheme/free item rules
- Product active/inactive status

Why it matters:
As product count grows, names alone are not enough for accurate stock, pricing, and reporting.


12. Security Hardening

The system has authentication and roles, but production security can be stronger.

Missing:
- Password reset
- Email verification
- Login throttling
- Account lockout after failed attempts
- Two-factor authentication
- Session list
- Revoke session
- Strong password policy
- Device/browser tracking

Why it matters:
Business systems contain sensitive financial and customer data.


13. Reporting Gaps

The system already has useful daily, monthly, finance, and profit reports. A real dealer system usually needs more.

Missing:
- Customer due report
- DSR due aging report
- Supplier due report
- Stock ledger report
- Stock valuation report
- Purchase report
- Sales invoice report
- Collection report by payment method
- Cash book report
- Bank book report
- Damage/loss report
- Product-wise sales trend
- Route-wise sales trend
- Closing report


Recommended Development Priority
--------------------------------

Priority 1: Financial and Stock Correctness
- Add stock movement ledger
- Add supplier and purchase receive workflow
- Add automatic DSR/customer due ledger
- Capture historical cost at sale time

Priority 2: Daily Control
- Add cash/bank book
- Add daily closing
- Add settlement approval and locking
- Add soft delete and trash

Priority 3: Sales Network
- Add customer/outlet management
- Add route planning
- Add customer-wise sales and due tracking

Priority 4: Documents and Reports
- Add invoice/challan/receipt numbering
- Add stock ledger report
- Add due aging reports
- Add cash/bank reports

Priority 5: Growth Features
- Add DSR targets and commission
- Add supplier due management
- Add product barcode/SKU/batch/expiry
- Add advanced security features


Final Assessment
----------------
The current system is usable for controlled daily DSR issue, settlement, stock, expense, and finance operations.

It is not yet a full dealer ERP/accounting system.

The biggest current risk is financial correctness over time. Stock, dues, cash, and profit should move toward ledger-based records instead of relying mainly on summary fields and manual entries.

Best next step:
Build the stock movement ledger and automatic due ledger before adding more UI-heavy features.

STOCKLEDGER PUBLIC MARKETING IMAGE GENERATION BRIEF
===================================================

Audit date: 2026-07-15
Scope: All public and pre-login pages in the current frontend.


1. WHAT THE AUDIT FOUND
-----------------------

The website has 40 pre-login routes:

- 38 public/SEO routes.
- 2 authentication routes.
- 28 SEO detail routes currently show a large marketing image in the hero.
- The Features, Solutions, and Software hub pages reuse those detail images as cards.
- The landing page reuses a smaller shared image library across many sections.

The current code reuses only about 15 marketing images across many unrelated pages. For example, the same general image is used for HR, repair, business management, and other different intentions. This saves files, but it makes pages look repetitive and sometimes makes the image weakly related to the page topic.

Recommended approach:

- Give every one of the 28 SEO detail pages its own relevant master image.
- Reuse each detail image on its matching hub card. Do not create separate hub-card images.
- Reuse selected feature and solution images on the landing page where the subject matches.
- Create only three additional landing/support masters: L01, L02, and C01.
- Use real photographs for the founder and named customer testimonials. Do not generate fictional people and present them as real customers.

Recommended total:

- 31 generated/composited marketing masters.
- 1 real founder portrait.
- 6 real customer portraits with permission.


2. NON-NEGOTIABLE VISUAL SYSTEM
-------------------------------

Use these rules for every generated image so the website feels like one brand.

Brand and color:

- Primary deep violet: #3C2A86.
- Dark violet: #2D2765.
- Accent teal: #0D9488.
- Backgrounds: warm white, pale grey, soft slate, natural wood, and realistic business interiors.
- Violet and teal should appear as small accents in UI, folders, packaging tape, clothing details, or decor. Do not cover the entire photograph in purple.

Photography direction:

- Premium but believable editorial photography of real Bangladeshi small and medium businesses.
- Adult Bangladeshi people, natural brown skin tones, realistic facial features, realistic hands, and normal body proportions.
- Natural window light or clean commercial daylight, gentle contrast, realistic textures, and restrained depth of field.
- Eye-level 35 mm or 50 mm camera feeling. Avoid extreme wide-angle distortion.
- Environments must look recognizably South Asian/Bangladeshi without using flags, monuments, stereotypes, or decorative cliches.
- Shelves, cartons, medicine packs, tools, receipts, scanners, thermal printers, and counters should look operational and organized, not like a luxury showroom.

People and clothing:

- All people must clearly be adults, generally 25-50 years old.
- Women: modest professional Bangladeshi clothing. Use long-sleeve salwar kameez or long kurta, full-length trousers, and a neatly draped dupatta when specified. No sleeveless tops, low necklines, tight clothing, transparent fabric, heavy glamour makeup, or fashion-model posing.
- Men: clean collared shirt, polo, modest panjabi, or business-casual shirt with full-length trousers. No suits unless the scene specifically calls for an executive office.
- Keep clothing colors realistic and varied: teal, navy, sage, maroon, muted mustard, cream, charcoal, olive, and pale blue.
- Do not make every woman wear a hijab. Where a hijab is specified, keep it simple, professional, and naturally styled.

Composition and crop safety:

- Standard landscape master: 1600 x 1000 px, 16:10.
- Current hub cards use 16:10 and current detail heroes use 16:11. Keep all faces, devices, and important objects inside the central 75 percent so the same master can crop safely for both.
- Keep at least 8 percent clear space on every edge.
- Do not place critical details in corners.
- The route hero displays the image on the right side of a dark background, so use a reasonably bright image with a clean edge and clear focal point.
- Export in sRGB as high-quality WebP. Keep a lossless PNG master when the image contains product UI.

Universal exclusions for all generated images:

- No visible third-party logos or trademarks.
- No invented StockLedger logo. Add the real logo later if needed.
- No watermark, caption, headline, labels, random letters, fake Bengali writing, or promotional text inside photographs.
- No floating holograms, sci-fi dashboards, neon cyberpunk effects, glowing charts in the air, giant coins, handshakes, call-center stock-photo poses, or generic Western office towers.
- No impossible shelves, duplicated products, deformed fingers, extra limbs, duplicate people, or unreadable device interfaces.
- No Indian rupee symbol. If currency is visible in a real screenshot, it should use the product's actual BDT formatting.


3. IMPORTANT RULE FOR DASHBOARDS AND PRODUCT UI
------------------------------------------------

Do not ask an image model to invent the StockLedger interface. Generated dashboards usually contain fake features, incorrect numbers, and broken text. That damages product trust.

For every prompt marked PRODUCT COMPOSITE:

1. Open the real StockLedger page in a desktop browser at approximately 1440 x 900.
2. Use realistic demo data with no private customer information.
3. Capture the exact screenshots listed in the brief.
4. Upload those screenshots to ChatGPT with the prompt.
5. The screenshots are immutable source artwork: preserve their text, numbers, spacing, colors, and layout exactly.
6. ChatGPT may create the background, browser frame, shadows, and composition only. It must not redraw the UI.

If ChatGPT cannot preserve the attached UI exactly, generate only the background/frame and composite the real screenshot afterward in an editor.
